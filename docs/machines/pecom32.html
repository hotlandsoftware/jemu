The PECOM 32 is a Yugoslavic computer released in 1985 by Ei Niš and RIZ Zagreb.

The PECOM was sold to local schools to teach IT/Computer Science to students. Notably, unlike many other computers in the Eastern Block, this machine used an imported CDP1802 processor instead of an 8080 or Z80 since Yugoslavia had somewhat better market conditions and could import them from the US. The PECOM32 has 32 KB of RAM, 16 KB of ROM, and has a BASIC interpreter built in.
